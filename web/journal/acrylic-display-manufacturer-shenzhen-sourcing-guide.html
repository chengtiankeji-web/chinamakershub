Acrylic Display Manufacturer in Shenzhen — Sourcing Guide

Everything a retail buyer or brand owner needs to know before sourcing custom acrylic display stands from Shenzhen.

Shenzhen is the primary sourcing hub for custom acrylic display stands. The city's concentration of plastic fabrication factories, sheet suppliers, and laser cutting specialists means faster turnaround, lower tooling costs, and more finishing options than sourcing from other Chinese cities.

Most acrylic display manufacturers in Shenzhen are located in Bao'an and Longhua districts, close to raw material suppliers. A typical factory can cut, bend, bond, print, and package acrylic components without outsourcing any process step — which reduces lead time and quality risk significantly.

The primary buyer categories for Shenzhen acrylic display manufacturers are cosmetics and beauty brands (counter displays and testers), electronics and 3C accessories (retail packaging and shelf fixtures), vape and pod device brands (display cases and charging stands), and eyewear and jewellery retailers (lockable showcases and risers).

What Acrylic Display Factories in Shenzhen Make

A typical Shenzhen acrylic display factory produces across several product categories. Counter display stands are the most common — custom-shaped acrylic risers, stepped displays, and branded counter units for beauty and skincare products.

Lockable display cases are produced for high-value items including electronics, vape devices, and jewellery. These typically use 5mm or 8mm cast acrylic with metal hinges and locks. Custom branding via UV printing, laser engraving, or screen printing is standard.

Retail shelf fixtures include gravity-fed dispensers, pegboard inserts, and modular shelving systems for fashion accessories. Trade show and exhibition displays — portable, lightweight acrylic structures that flat-pack for shipping — are a growing category, particularly for brands attending international trade shows.

MOQ and Lead Times

Minimum order quantities for custom acrylic displays in Shenzhen are generally lower than buyers expect. Most factories accept orders from 50 to 200 pieces for standard configurations. For fully custom designs with unique tooling, MOQ is typically 100 to 500 pieces depending on complexity.

Sample lead time is 7 to 15 days from approved drawing. Production lead time for orders under 500 pieces is typically 15 to 25 days. Larger orders over 1,000 pieces run 25 to 35 days. These timelines assume approved artwork and materials in stock — delays in artwork approval are the most common cause of extended lead times.

The OEM Process — From Reference to Production

Sourcing a custom acrylic display from a Shenzhen factory follows a predictable process. The first step is providing a reference — this can be a drawing, a sketch, a photo of an existing display, or a sample to replicate. Most factories do not require a technical CAD drawing; a clear image and approximate dimensions are sufficient to generate a quotation.

The factory produces a dimensioned drawing for approval before proceeding to sampling. This drawing stage typically takes 2 to 3 working days. Once the drawing is approved, a physical sample is produced and shipped for review. Sample approval can require one or two rounds of revision, which is normal — particularly for displays requiring tight dimensional tolerances or complex print registration.

After sample approval, the buyer places a production deposit (typically 30%) and production begins. The balance is paid before shipment. Most Shenzhen factories ship FOB Shenzhen or can arrange door-to-door freight via freight forwarder.

What to Check Before Placing an Order

Material grade is the first thing to verify. Ask whether the factory uses cast acrylic (better optical clarity, more consistent thickness, easier to machine) or extruded acrylic (lower cost, slightly less clear, more prone to stress crazing over time). For premium retail displays, cast acrylic is the correct specification.

Bonding method matters for structural integrity. Solvent bonding (using methylene chloride) produces near-invisible joins and is stronger than mechanical assembly. Ask the factory to confirm their bonding process for structural joints. Poor bonding is the most common quality complaint in this category.

UV printing resolution should be confirmed if you require printed branding. Ask for a printed sample on acrylic before approving production — screen printing and UV printing produce different results in terms of colour accuracy and durability.

Finally, confirm whether the factory holds any relevant certifications. For products entering the EU or US market, RoHS compliance for any electronic components (LED lighting integrated into displays) is required. BSCI social audits are increasingly requested by European retail buyers.
