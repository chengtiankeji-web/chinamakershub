Foshan Furniture Manufacturing: The Complete Buyer's Guide

Foshan produces 30% of China's furniture exports. Here's how to navigate its districts, trade shows, and factory tiers.

Foshan is the undisputed centre of China's furniture manufacturing industry. Located 30km southwest of Guangzhou in Guangdong province, it accounts for an estimated 30% of China's furniture exports and houses over 5,000 furniture manufacturers across its districts.

For buyers sourcing furniture for retail, hospitality, contract or residential applications, Foshan is unavoidable. But navigating it effectively requires knowing where to go — and what to avoid.

The Key Districts

Lecong / Longjiang (乐从/龙江) — The world's largest furniture wholesale market. Lecong alone houses over 3,000 showrooms across multiple massive market complexes. This is where trading companies and manufacturers display their ranges. Useful for product discovery; less useful for direct factory access.

Shunde (顺德) — High-end furniture, particularly upholstered pieces and European-style designs. Many of Foshan's most capable manufacturers — including OEM suppliers for European brands — are based here.

Nanhai (南海) — Aluminium profiles, hardware components, metal furniture frames. If your products involve aluminium or metal sub-components, Nanhai is the sourcing hub.

Chancheng (禅城) — The city centre. Mix of furniture, ceramics and building materials. More mixed than the specialist districts.

Trade Shows Worth Attending

CIFF (China International Furniture Fair) — Held twice yearly in Guangzhou and Foshan. One of the world's largest furniture trade shows. The Foshan edition (typically September) skews more heavily toward manufacturing capability rather than finished consumer product.

Furniture China (Shanghai) — Smaller than CIFF but with strong international buyer attendance. Useful for benchmarking global price points.

A note on trade shows: they're excellent for identifying suppliers and products but are a poor substitute for factory visits. Many trade show exhibitors in Foshan are wholesalers or trading companies representing multiple factories. Always confirm manufacturer identity before assuming you've found a direct factory.

Local KnowledgeThe best Foshan factories often don't exhibit at trade shows at all. Their order books are full from existing relationships. Finding them requires introductions or platforms with direct factory access.

Factory Tiers in Foshan

Foshan's furniture manufacturers operate across several quality and capability tiers:

  • Tier 1 — Export-focused OEM suppliers: ISO certified, experienced with European standards and certifications (CARB, FSC, EN 16139), able to handle complex documentation requirements. These factories supply directly to IKEA, Ashley, Restoration Hardware and similar. High MOQs, longer lead times, but reliable quality.
  • Tier 2 — Mid-market manufacturers: Solid production capability, less experienced with complex export compliance. Good quality at competitive prices; suitable for most mid-market buyers.
  • Tier 3 — Small workshops: Often highly skilled for specific categories (e.g., solid wood joinery, hand-finishing) but with limited capacity, inconsistent QC systems, and less sophisticated export capability. Best for low-volume, high-craft requirements.

Common Mistakes Buyers Make in Foshan

  • Buying from a showroom without confirming it's a manufacturer (many are traders)
  • Judging factory quality by showroom aesthetics — a beautiful showroom can front a poor production facility
  • Not specifying CARB or REACH compliance upfront for products containing wood panels — retrofitting certification is expensive
  • Underestimating lead times for first orders with new factories: 60–90 days for production is common
