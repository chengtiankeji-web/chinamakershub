**章节1：Why Shenzhen for Acrylic Displays**

Shenzhen is the primary sourcing hub for custom acrylic display stands. The city's concentration of plastic fabrication factories, sheet suppliers, and laser cutting specialists means faster turnaround, lower tooling costs, and more finishing options than sourcing from other Chinese cities.

Most acrylic display manufacturers in Shenzhen are located in Bao'an and Longhua districts, close to raw material suppliers. A typical factory can cut, bend, bond, print, and package acrylic components without outsourcing any process step — which reduces lead time and quality risk significantly.

The primary buyer categories for Shenzhen acrylic display manufacturers are cosmetics and beauty brands (counter displays and testers), electronics and 3C accessories (retail packaging and shelf fixtures), vape and pod device brands (display cases and charging stands), and eyewear and jewellery retailers (lockable showcases and risers).

**章节2：What Acrylic Display Factories in Shenzhen Make**

A typical Shenzhen acrylic display factory produces across several product categories. Counter display stands are the most common — custom-shaped acrylic risers, stepped displays, and branded counter units for beauty and skincare products.

Lockable display cases are produced for high-value items including electronics, vape devices, and jewellery. These typically use 5mm or 8mm cast acrylic with metal hinges and locks. Custom branding via UV printing, laser engraving, or screen printing is standard.

Retail shelf fixtures include gravity-fed dispensers, pegboard inserts, and modular shelving systems for fashion accessories. Trade show and exhibition displays — portable, lightweight acrylic structures that flat-pack for shipping — are a growing category, particularly for brands attending international trade shows.

**章节3：MOQ and Lead Times**

Minimum order quantities for custom acrylic displays in Shenzhen are generally lower than buyers expect. Most factories accept orders from 50 to 200 pieces for standard configurations. For fully custom designs with unique tooling, MOQ is typically 100 to 500 pieces depending on complexity.

Sample lead time is 7 to 15 days from approved drawing. Production lead time for orders under 500 pieces is typically 15 to 25 days. Larger orders over 1,000 pieces run 25 to 35 days. These timelines assume approved artwork and materials in stock — delays in artwork approval are the most common cause of extended lead times.

<a href="/factories/yixinheng-acrylic/" style="color:var(--cinnabar);font-weight:500;">Yixinheng Acrylic</a> in Bao'an, Shenzhen, for example, accepts OEM orders from 500 pieces with a 35-day production lead time — a typical benchmark for a mid-size verified factory in this category.

**章节4：The OEM Process — From Reference to Production**

Sourcing a custom acrylic display from a Shenzhen factory follows a predictable process. The first step is providing a reference — this can be a drawing, a sketch, a photo of an existing display, or a sample to replicate. Most factories do not require a technical CAD drawing; a clear image and approximate dimensions are sufficient to generate a quotation.

The factory produces a dimensioned drawing for approval before proceeding to sampling. This drawing stage typically takes 2 to 3 working days. Once the drawing is approved, a physical sample is produced and shipped for review. Sample approval can require one or two rounds of revision, which is normal — particularly for displays requiring tight dimensional tolerances or complex print registration.

After sample approval, the buyer places a production deposit (typically 30%) and production begins. The balance is paid before shipment. Most Shenzhen factories ship FOB Shenzhen or can arrange door-to-door freight via freight forwarder.

**章节5：What to Check Before Placing an Order**

Material grade is the first thing to verify. Ask whether the factory uses cast acrylic (better optical clarity, more consistent thickness, easier to machine) or extruded acrylic (lower cost, slightly less clear, more prone to stress crazing over time). For premium retail displays, cast acrylic is the correct specification.

Bonding method matters for structural integrity. Solvent bonding (using methylene chloride) produces near-invisible joins and is stronger than mechanical assembly. Ask the factory to confirm their bonding process for structural joints. Poor bonding is the most common quality complaint in this category.

UV printing resolution should be confirmed if you require printed branding. Ask for a printed sample on acrylic before approving production — screen printing and UV printing produce different results in terms of colour accuracy and durability.

Finally, confirm whether the factory holds any relevant certifications. For products entering the EU or US market, RoHS compliance for any electronic components (LED lighting integrated into displays) is required. BSCI social audits are increasingly requested by European retail buyers.

**章节1：Why Zhongshan for LED Lighting**

Zhongshan's Guzhen district is the acknowledged centre of China's lighting manufacturing industry, producing an estimated 70% of the country's decorative and commercial lighting. The concentration of component suppliers, tooling shops, and finished goods manufacturers in a single geographic area creates cost and lead time advantages that other Chinese cities cannot match.

For commercial LED buyers — warehouses, retail chains, hotels, and electrical contractors — Zhongshan offers factories across every specification tier, from basic commodity LED panels to UL and DLC certified commercial luminaires. The key sourcing challenge is not finding manufacturers, but identifying which factories hold genuine third-party certifications and have the quality systems to maintain consistency across repeat orders.

**章节2：Product Categories**

Commercial LED manufacturers in Zhongshan cover three primary categories. High-bay and industrial lighting includes UFO high-bays from 100W to 500W, linear high-bays, and low-bays for retail and warehouse applications. The primary certification requirements for this category are UL 1598 and DLC Premium listing for the North American market, and CE marking for Europe.

Linear and panel lighting covers LED troffers, batten fixtures, and panel lights for commercial interiors. DLC listing is particularly important for this category in the US market, as many utility rebate programmes require DLC certification. UGR ratings below 19 are increasingly specified for office and educational environments.

Outdoor and architectural lighting includes flood lights, street lights, and garden luminaires. IP66 weatherproofing and IK10 impact resistance are the standard specifications for outdoor commercial use. CE and ENEC marking are required for European projects; SAA certification for Australia.

**章节3：Certification — What Matters for Your Market**

Certification requirements vary significantly by destination market, and this is the area where sourcing errors are most costly.

For North America, UL listing under UL 1598 or UL 8750 is required for products entering the US and Canadian market through electrical contractors or major distributors. DLC (DesignLights Consortium) listing is separate from UL — it is an energy efficiency qualification required for rebate programme eligibility. Factories can hold UL without DLC, and vice versa. Buyers targeting utility rebate channels should confirm DLC Premium listing specifically.

For Europe and the UK, CE marking is the minimum requirement. ENEC certification, issued by European notified bodies, is a stronger credential and increasingly required by European electrical wholesalers and specification channels. LM-80 photometric testing of the LED source is required for products claiming a rated lumen output.

For Australia and New Zealand, SAA (Standards Australia) certification is required. This is separate from CE and is often overlooked by buyers who assume European certification transfers. <a href="/factories/haoguang-led/" style="color:var(--cinnabar);font-weight:500;">Haoguang LED</a> in Zhongshan holds UL, DLC Premium, CE, and SAA certifications across its commercial high-bay range — a relatively complete certification set for a factory of its scale.

**章节4：MOQ and Lead Times**

MOQ for commercial LED products is generally lower than buyers expect, particularly for standard catalogue items. Most Zhongshan factories accept orders from 50 to 200 pieces for items held in semi-finished stock. Custom configurations — custom colour temperatures, custom housings, or private label — typically require 200 to 500 pieces.

Sample lead time is 7 to 14 days. Production lead time for orders under 500 pieces is 25 to 35 days. Certification testing adds time if a new product variant requires independent lab testing — budget an additional 4 to 6 weeks for UL or DLC certification of a new SKU.

**章节5：What to Check Before Ordering**

Request the actual certification certificate, not just a factory claim. UL listed products can be verified on the UL Product iQ database at iq.ul.com. DLC listed products can be verified at designlights.org. CE declarations of conformity should name the issuing notified body. Any factory unable to provide verifiable certification documentation should be treated with caution.

Lumen output claims should be supported by LM-80 test data from an independent photometric laboratory. Inflated lumen claims are common in the commodity LED market — verify published lumens against an independent test report before specifying.

Ask for colour rendering index (CRI) data at the specified colour temperature. For retail and hospitality applications, CRI above 90 is typically required. Many factories default to CRI 80 unless specified otherwise.

## 正文（6个章节）

**章节1：Why Source Door Hardware from China**

China — and Foshan in particular — produces the majority of the world's architectural door hardware. Foshan's hardware manufacturing cluster, centred around Nanhai and Shunde districts, has been producing hinges, handles, locks, and closers for international markets for over three decades. The cost advantage over European and North American manufactured hardware is significant, particularly for mid-range and commercial-grade specification.

The key challenge for buyers in the US, UK, Australian, and European markets is certification. Door hardware for commercial and residential construction projects must meet specific standards — ANSI/BHMA in North America, EN standards in Europe, and AS 4145 in Australia. Not all Chinese factories hold these certifications, and some factories make claims that do not withstand verification.

**章节2：ANSI/BHMA Certification Explained**

ANSI/BHMA (American National Standards Institute / Builders Hardware Manufacturers Association) certification is the primary standard for architectural hardware in the US and Canadian markets. The relevant standards are ANSI/BHMA A156.1 for butts and hinges, A156.2 for locks and lock trim, A156.4 for door closers, and A156.13 for mortise locks.

Each standard has three grades. Grade 1 is the highest, intended for heavy commercial use. Grade 2 is for light commercial and residential applications. Grade 3 is residential only. For commercial construction projects, Grade 1 is typically specified.

ANSI/BHMA certified products are listed in the BHMA Certified Products Directory, which is publicly searchable at bhma.org. This is the most reliable way to verify a Chinese factory's ANSI certification claim — if the product is not in the BHMA directory, the certification claim cannot be relied upon.

**章节3：EN and CE Certification for Europe and UK**

European door hardware certification falls under several EN standards. EN 1906 covers lever handles and knob furniture. EN 1154 covers hydraulic door closers. EN 12209 covers mechanically operated locks and latches.

CE marking is required for door hardware placed on the European market. The CE mark must be accompanied by a Declaration of Performance (DoP) that identifies the notified body that assessed the product and lists the declared performance characteristics. Buyers should request the DoP and verify the notified body identification number against the NANDO database (nando.ec.europa.eu).

For the UK market post-Brexit, UKCA marking is technically required, though the UK government has extended the acceptance of CE marking for construction products. Buyers specifying for UK projects should confirm current requirements with their clients.

**章节4：AS 4145 for Australia and New Zealand**

AS 4145 is the Australian Standard for locksets and hardware. It covers mechanical performance, security rating, and finish durability. Products certified to AS 4145 must be tested by a NATA-accredited laboratory or an internationally recognised equivalent.

AS 4145 certification is separate from ANSI and EN certification and is frequently overlooked by buyers who assume European certification is accepted in Australia. It is not. Buyers sourcing for Australian projects should confirm AS 4145 certification specifically.

<a href="/factories/dahua-door-hardware/" style="color:var(--cinnabar);font-weight:500;">Dahua Door Hardware</a> in Foshan holds ANSI/BHMA certification for its hinge and handle ranges, CE marking to EN 1906 and EN 1154, and AS 4145 certification for its lockset range — one of the more complete multi-market certification sets among Chinese door hardware manufacturers.

**章节5：PVD and Surface Finish Durability**

Surface finish durability is one of the most common quality complaints in imported door hardware. Electroplated finishes — chrome, satin nickel, antique brass — can fail prematurely if the plating chemistry is incorrect or the substrate is not properly prepared.

PVD (Physical Vapour Deposition) coating is significantly more durable than electroplating. PVD matte black, brushed gold, and satin brass finishes typically achieve Vickers hardness above 2,000 HV and salt-spray resistance exceeding 720 hours. Electroplated equivalents typically achieve 200 to 400 hours salt-spray resistance.

Ask factories for salt-spray test certificates for the specific finish you are ordering. Certificates should state the test duration, the test standard (ASTM B117 or ISO 9227), and the result. Factories without documented salt-spray test data are a higher risk for finish failures in service.

**章节1：The MOQ Question Every Buyer Asks**

Minimum order quantity is the first question most buyers ask when approaching Foshan furniture manufacturers, and the answer is rarely a single number. MOQ in Foshan furniture manufacturing is highly variable — it depends on the product category, the factory's scale and production model, the degree of customisation required, and whether the buyer is asking for catalogue items or bespoke designs.

The most common misconception is that all Foshan factories require large minimum orders. This is not accurate. MOQ ranges from 10 units for some upholstered furniture OEM factories to 1,000 units or more for high-volume commodity manufacturers. Understanding which type of factory you are dealing with determines what MOQ you can realistically negotiate.

**章节2：MOQ by Product Category**

Upholstered furniture — sofas, armchairs, and beds — generally has the lowest MOQ among Foshan furniture categories. Because upholstery is labour-intensive rather than material-intensive, factories can produce small runs without significant setup cost. MOQ for upholstered sofas and beds at OEM-focused factories is typically 10 to 50 units per SKU. <a href="/factories/gostoo-furniture/" style="color:var(--cinnabar);font-weight:500;">Gostoo Furniture</a> in Foshan, for example, accepts sofa orders from 10 units — practical for boutique retailers, interior designers, and Airbnb operators placing their first order.

Solid wood furniture — dining tables, bedroom sets, and occasional pieces — has a higher MOQ due to material procurement and machining setup costs. Typical MOQ for solid wood furniture from a mid-size Foshan factory is 100 to 500 units per SKU. FSC certified teak pieces, which require specific timber purchasing, typically require 200 units minimum.

Office furniture and commercial seating carries MOQs in the 50 to 200 unit range for standard configurations. Custom dimensions or non-standard finishes typically double the MOQ. Metal furniture and retail fixtures, due to tooling and powder coat line setup, generally requires 200 units minimum.

**章节3：What Drives MOQ Higher**

Custom tooling is the primary driver of high MOQ requirements. If your design requires custom-cut components, new moulds, or bespoke hardware, the factory will amortise tooling costs across the production run — which pushes MOQ up. Understanding whether your design requires custom tooling, or whether it can be produced from existing factory components and hardware, significantly affects the MOQ you will be quoted.

Fabric and material procurement is the second factor. For upholstered furniture, if you specify a COM (Customer's Own Material) fabric, there is no minimum fabric order constraint from the factory side — you supply the fabric. If you ask the factory to source a specific fabric, they may require a minimum run to justify the fabric purchase. This is negotiable if the fabric is a standard trade reference held by major Foshan fabric suppliers.

Finish options affect MOQ for metal and hardware components. Powder coat colours outside the factory's standard palette require a minimum batch for the coating run, typically adding 100 to 200 units to the MOQ. PVD finishes have similar constraints.

**章节4：How to Negotiate MOQ**

The most effective MOQ negotiation approach is to accept the factory's standard MOQ on your first order, with a clear commitment to repeat. Factories evaluate buyer reliability based on payment history and reorder frequency. A buyer who places a clean first order — clear brief, timely approval, prompt payment — will typically find the factory more flexible on MOQ for subsequent orders.

Consolidating SKUs is another effective approach. Rather than ordering 20 units each of five different sofas, ordering 100 units of one model reaches MOQ more efficiently. You can diversify your range in later orders once the supplier relationship is established.

For buyers who cannot reach standard MOQ, an alternative is to work with a Foshan sourcing agent who consolidates orders from multiple buyers to reach MOQ. This approach works for catalogue items but is less suitable for custom designs, where quality accountability is more important.

**章节5：Sample Orders and the First Order Process**

Most Foshan furniture factories accept sample orders before committing to production. Sample lead time is 15 to 30 days depending on product complexity. Sample cost is typically charged at the standard unit cost plus a setup fee — expect to pay 1.5 to 3 times the production unit cost for a single sample.

The sample approval process is important. Be specific about what you are approving — dimensions, finish, fabric, and hardware. Vague sample approval feedback leads to production disputes. A clear written approval noting any deviations from the original specification, and any amendments agreed for production, protects both buyer and factory.
